Bangalore ⚠
⚠ Bangalore last in occupancy, revenue AND ratings — systemic failure requiring urgent review.
Based on analysis of 134,590 bookings across 25 properties in 4 cities — May to July 2022
🔴 Urgent — Revenue Growth
Launch Weekday Corporate Packages
Weekday occupancy at 51.81% vs 73.96% weekend — a 22 point gap. Hotels are half empty Monday–Thursday. Target corporate travellers in Delhi, Mumbai and Hyderabad with dedicated B2B packages, corporate rates, and loyalty programmes.
↑ Estimated impact: ₹150–200M additional annual revenue at +10% weekday occupancy
🔴 Urgent — Pricing Strategy
Review Delhi Room Rates Immediately
Delhi has the highest occupancy (61.51%) but generates the least revenue (₹294M). Rooms are being sold too cheaply. Occupancy is strong enough to support a 15–20% room rate increase without losing demand.
↑ A 15% rate increase in Delhi alone could add ₹40–50M in annual revenue
🔴 Urgent — Brand Performance
Operational Audit of Bangalore Properties
Bangalore ranked last in occupancy, revenue AND ratings consistently. This is not a location problem — it is a systemic operational failure. Review staffing, pricing, maintenance, and competitive positioning in the Bangalore market.
↑ Closing 50% of the gap with Mumbai could recover ₹120M+ in revenue
🟡 Medium — Channel Strategy
Direct Booking Loyalty Programme
Direct online and offline channels generate only ₹255M combined vs ₹699M from "Others". Every third-party booking incurs commission costs. Introduce tangible direct booking incentives — loyalty points, free breakfast, room upgrades.
↑ Shifting 10% of bookings to direct could save ₹15–20M in annual commissions
🟡 Medium — Data Quality
Classify "Others" Booking Category
"Others" is the single largest revenue source at ₹699M — 41% of total revenue. Management cannot make channel strategy decisions without knowing where this revenue comes from. Immediate classification and audit required.
↑ Better channel visibility enables smarter marketing spend allocation
🟡 Medium — Guest Experience
Minimum Rating Standard of 3.5
Atliq Seasons Mumbai (2.30), Grands Bangalore (2.36), and Exotica Hyderabad (2.33) are all below 2.5 — damaging booking conversion on third-party platforms. Implement quarterly rating reviews with mandatory improvement plans for properties below 3.5.
↑ Ratings below 3.0 reduce platform visibility and booking conversion by est. 20–30%
If only one action is taken
Fix weekday occupancy through corporate packages and dynamic pricing.
A 10 percentage point improvement in weekday occupancy across all 25 properties would generate an estimated ₹150–200M in additional annual revenue — the single highest-impact action available to management.
